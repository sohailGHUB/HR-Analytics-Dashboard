# Dashboard Insights

## Project Summary

This dashboard provides an overview of employee attrition and workforce demographics using the IBM HR Analytics dataset. 
It helps analyze attrition patterns across departments, job roles, age groups, salary slabs, overtime, and years at the company.


# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| KPI | Value |
|------|-------|
| Total Employees | 1480 |
| Employees Left | 238 |
| Attrition Rate | 16.1% |
| Average Age | 37 Years |
| Average Monthly Salary | 6.5K |
| Average Years at Company | 7.01 Years |

---

# Dashboard Components

## KPI Cards

The dashboard displays the following key metrics:

- Total Employees
- Employees Left (Attrition Count)
- Attrition Rate
- Average Age
- Average Salary
- Average Years at Company


## Attrition by Education

This visualization shows employee attrition across different education backgrounds.

Purpose:
- Identify education groups with higher employee turnover.


## Attrition by Age Group

This chart compares attrition across different age groups.

Purpose:
- Identify the age group most affected by attrition.


## Attrition by Job Role

Displays employee attrition for each job role.

Purpose:
- Identify roles with higher employee turnover.


## Attrition by Salary Slab

Shows attrition across different salary ranges.

Purpose:
- Understand whether salary has an impact on employee attrition.


## Attrition by Overtime

Compares attrition between employees who worked overtime and those who did not.

Purpose:
- Analyze the relationship between overtime and employee turnover.


## Attrition by Years at Company

Shows employee attrition based on years spent in the organization.

Purpose:
- Understand employee retention over time.


## Attrition Matrix

Displays attrition count by Job Role and Job Level.

Purpose:
- Compare attrition across different job roles and hierarchy levels.


# Key Findings

- Total employee attrition is **238** out of **1480** employees.
- Overall attrition rate is **16.1%**.
- Laboratory Technicians experienced the highest attrition.
- Employees earning below **5K** showed the highest employee turnover.
- Employees working overtime had noticeably higher attrition.
- Employees in the **26–35** age group contributed the highest number of attrition cases.
- Attrition was highest during the early years of employment.
